I am a computational researcher specializing in high-performance computing (HPC) and computational fluid dynamics (CFD). My work focuses on bridging complex physical simulations with modern GPU acceleration techniques.

Currently, I am investigating transition-sensitive RANS models and hybrid RANS-LES methods for turbomachinery applications, specifically analyzing flow separation in turbine cascades. Alongside this, I actively develop and optimize computational solvers, leveraging tools like NVIDIA Nsight, Warp, and CuPy to achieve maximum TFLOPs throughput via memory restructuring and GPU parallelization. 

Beyond core fluid dynamics, I am deeply interested in Scientific Machine Learning (SciML) and the intersection of neural networks with physical solver environments.

Feel free to explore my [projects](/projects/) to see some of my simulation videos, validation cases, and optimization workflows.
