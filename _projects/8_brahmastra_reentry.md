During my internship as a Project Design Intern at **Brahmastra Aerospace Systems**, I focused on the extreme thermal and aerodynamic challenges associated with atmospheric re-entry[cite: 1].

### CAD Modeling & High-Temperature CFD
I was responsible for the Computer-Aided Design (CAD) modeling of the Re-entry Vehicle[cite: 1]. Once the geometry was established, I utilized **Ansys Fluent Density-Based Solvers** to simulate the hypersonic flow conditions experienced during the re-entry phase[cite: 1].

The primary goal of these simulations was to visualize the extreme high-temperature regions generated across the vehicle's surface[cite: 1]. By accurately mapping the thermal loads, I was able to guide the selection of appropriate shielding materials necessary to protect the capsule's interior, sensitive instruments, and sensors from thermal degradation[cite: 1].
