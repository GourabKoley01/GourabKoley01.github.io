### Optimizing Supersonic Combustion
A major focus of my research has been the iterative design of scramjet combustors to solve the critical issue of short flow residence times at supersonic speeds. 

Using **SolidWorks** for CAD modeling and **Ansys Fluent** for computational fluid dynamics analysis, I investigated multiple techniques to induce turbulent flow patterns within the combustion chamber[cite: 1]. By strategically creating turbulence amidst the shocks, the fluid-fuel mixing is significantly enhanced, leading to better fuel efficiency and a higher exit Mach number[cite: 1].

### The Three-Cavity Design & Geometric Variations
One of my primary breakthroughs was the development of a three-wall cavity scramjet combustor[cite: 1]. The addition of a third cavity creates an essential extra space for the combustion of unburnt fuel and air, drastically improving the residence time[cite: 1]. 

I expanded this research by conducting a trade-off study on the combustor's geometry, analyzing how length and exit angle variations affect weight and efficiency[cite: 1]. I discovered that a 10% increment in both length and angle yielded the most favorable results for optimizing combustor weight[cite: 1]. I presented these findings at the international conference AMCES 2023 and the National Conference on Propulsion and Aerospace Engineering (NCPCAE'24)[cite: 1].

### Strut Modifications & Recognition
Furthering this work, I researched unique strut modifications to increase temperature and pressure at the rear of the strut, optimizing unburnt fuel combustion[cite: 1]. 

The success of the three-wall cavity design led to the filing of a design patent under the Intellectual Property Rights of India (Application No. 202311048961), as well as a journal publication submission to the International Journal of Engine Research[cite: 1].
