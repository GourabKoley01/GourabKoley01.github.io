This project investigates flow separation characteristics in turbomachinery, specifically analyzing the SPLEEN C1 turbine blade cascade. This work forms a core part of my master's thesis research.

### Computational Approach
Accurately predicting flow separation requires highly resolved turbulence modeling. I utilize the SU2 suite to configure and run transition-sensitive RANS models and hybrid RANS-LES methods, specifically Detached Eddy Simulations (DES). 

### Challenges Addressed
One of the primary numerical challenges in this setup involved solving persistent divergence issues associated with periodic boundaries in the cascade mesh. By refining the boundary treatments and solver configurations, I achieved stable residual convergence.
